My passion for tech started pretty simple: playing computer games as a kid and wondering how they actually worked. That curiosity stuck with me into college, where diving into hackathons during my bachelor's degree made me realize how much I loved building things from scratch. I did my master’s in computer science to dive deeper into the field, and today, I build large-scale applications that leverage the cloud for global financial platforms. I handle the entire software development lifecycle — from writing the core backend logic and designing the architecture to automating the DevOps pipelines. It’s been a really rewarding journey, turning a childhood hobby into a career focused on shipping reliable, secure products from start to finish.

I thrive in environments where the bar is set high, whether I'm architecting a major system rollout or volunteering to teach kids how to write their very first lines of code. For me, it's about staying grounded, working with a clear purpose, and bringing a sense of stewardship to everything I touch — always learning, exploring, and building things in a way that elevates the systems and sets up the team for long-term success.

Beyond the digital architectures and logic paths, my truest sense of wonder comes from the wild. It began early, exploring the ancient, misty ridges of the Western Ghats as a child. Years later, chasing the horizon on a bike into the high silences of the Himalayas, I watched the Milky Way spill across a freezing sky and saw desert sands, winter snows, and camels living together in the surreal valley of Hunder — a place where the warmth of the mountain people reminded me what it means to stay truly grounded. My travels have always been a search for that raw, untouched pulse of nature. I've stood on the massive, blue-ice expanses of glaciers in Kenai Fjords, shared quiet, breath-holding moments with grizzly bears in the wilderness of Katmai, and watched sudden, dramatic rain bursts fracture the desert sun in Big Bend. From the ever-cloudy, whispering canopy of the Great Smoky Mountains to the sun-drenched corals suspended in the impossibly clear waters of Biscayne, every trail, hike and the journey has been an exercise in perspective — a reminder of the vast, enduring, and timeless forces that shape our world.
